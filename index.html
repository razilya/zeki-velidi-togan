Zeki Velidi Togan

Bashkir historian, Turkologist and leader of the Bashkir revolutionary and liberation movement.

Here's a time line of Dr. Togan's life:

  • 1890 - born in Kuzyanovo (Bashkir: Көҙән) village of Sterlitamaksky Uyezd, Ufa Governorate (in present-day Ishimbaysky District, Bashkortostan).
  • 1915 - 1917 - was a member of bureau, supporting Muslim deputies at the State Duma.
  • 1917 - was elected to the Millät Mäclese, and with Şerif Manatov, he organized the Bashkir Shuro (Council).
  • February 1919 - June 1920 - he was chairman of the Bashrevkom (Bashkir Revolutionary Committee). However, feeling the Bolsheviks had broken their promises, he became more critical of them when he moved to Central Asia.
  • 1920 - 1923 - he was chairman of the "National Union of Turkistan".
  • 1923 - emigrated, after discovering original manuscripts of Ahmad ibn Fadlan in Iran.
  • From 1925 Velidi lived in Turkey.
  • 1927 - was appointed Chair of Turkish History at the Istanbul University.
  • 1935 - gained a doctor of philosophy at the University of Vienna.
  • 1935 - 1937 - became a professor at Bonn University.
  • 1938 - 1939 - became a professor at Göttingen University.
  • 1953 - became organizer of the İslam Tetkikleri Enstitüsü (Institute for Islamic Studies) at Istanbul University.
  • 1967 - was given an honorary doctorate from the University of Manchester.
  • 1970 - dies at the age of 79.
